Hi there! I'm a PhD student in the Motivated Cognition Lab and Brain, Attention, and Reality Lab at the University of British Columbia (department profile), working with Dr. Rebecca Todd and Dr. Alan Kingstone.

Currently, I'm interested in:

  • How people with varying levels of mood disorder symptoms deploy effort to avoid unpleasant outcomes and obtain rewards
  • What drives us to "work" or "slack" when making decisons about cognitive effort deployment for reward
  • How the anterior cingulate cortex represents information we learn about unpleasant and rewarding outcomes
  • How the context in which we listen to a sound can affect how we rate and describe the sound

Recently, I've also:

  • Developed software to automatically register and segment mesoscale calcium imaging data based on standardized atlases (MesoNet), and to track and reward animal behaviours in real time (DeepCut2RealTime), with Dr. Tim Murphy
  • Explored how steroid levels change with stress in mice, with Dr. Kiran Soma

I have programming experience with Python (PsychoPy, Django, TensorFlow, PyTorch, DeepLabCut), R, JavaScript, Bash (Linux, high performance computing including SLURM, PBS, and Azure), C# (Unity, .NET), and HTML/CSS. I've also worked extensively with fMRI preprocessing and analysis software, including fMRIprep, AFNI, FSL, tedana, CONN, and fMRI-CPCA, and I help organize and co-teach an annual, week-long fMRI brain imaging workshop at UBC.
